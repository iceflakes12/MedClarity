Osteoporosis Information

What is Osteoporosis?

Osteoporosis is a medical condition characterized by the weakening of bones, making them fragile and more prone to fractures. It occurs when the density and quality of bone are reduced.

Symptoms

  • Back pain, caused by a fractured or collapsed vertebra
  • Loss of height over time
  • A stooped posture
  • Frequent bone fractures, especially in the hip, spine, and wrist

Risk Factors

  • Age (increased risk with aging)
  • Gender (more common in women)
  • Family history of osteoporosis
  • Low body weight and small frame
  • Low estrogen levels in women and low testosterone levels in men
  • Insufficient calcium and vitamin D intake
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Smoking and excessive alcohol consumption

What You Can Do

  • Adequate calcium and vitamin D intake through diet or supplements
  • Regular weight-bearing and muscle-strengthening exercises
  • Medications to strengthen bones and reduce fracture risk
  • Healthy lifestyle choices, including quitting smoking and moderating alcohol intake
